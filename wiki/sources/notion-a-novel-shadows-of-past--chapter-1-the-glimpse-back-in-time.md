## Summary
In the solitude of the classroom, the only companionship I found was the lingering scent of chalk, a faint echo of the day's lessons, and the soft, gentle tapping of rain against the windows. It had the rhythm of an old song, familiar yet forgotten. I was ensconced at my desk, my gaze sweeping over the disarray of papers scattered before me. However, my mind was far away from the present moment, lost in the labyrinth of memories I could never quite comprehend, let alone attempt to navigate. In the silence of the room, it happened again. As though a switch had been flipped, the world around me blurred and distorted, the edges of reality fraying and fading like an aged, yellowed photograph left too long in the sun. My heart pounded in my chest like a wild drum, matching the frenetic tempo of my thoughts as I felt the now familiar sensation, akin to falling backward into an abyss, dark and deep. As the world settled and my heart regained its rhythm, I found myself no longer enclosed within the confines of my classroom. Instead, the air was heavy and thick with the unmistakable aroma of damp cobblestone and the faint, lingering traces of coal smoke. I was in Victorian London, a time and place teeming with life, sprawled out around me like an open book waiting to be read.
