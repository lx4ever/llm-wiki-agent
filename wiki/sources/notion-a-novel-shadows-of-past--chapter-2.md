## Summary
### Chapter 2: The Councilor's Intuition The next day found me in the local archives, the photograph of the boy and the councillor clutched in my hand. Among the musty pages and faded ink, I searched for any clue that might lead me to understand my time-traveling experiences. Hours passed until a name caught my eye: Lillian Grayson, a councillor known in the late Victorian era for her work with London's underprivileged children. The more I read, the more intrigued I became. She was a woman ahead of her time, fiercely intelligent and deeply empathetic.
