## Summary
The world outside the manor was shrouded in mist as I made my way through the wrought-iron gates. The image of Damien, isolated and brooding, lingered in my mind, a puzzle waiting to be solved. Over the next few weeks, as autumn leaves turned to gold and then to brown, I pieced together the fragments of Damien's story. Each visit to the manor revealed more of the tapestry of his life, each thread darker and more complex than the last. It was during an unguarded moment in the library, surrounded by ancient tomes and flickering candlelight, that I first heard of Damien's violent tendencies. Mrs. Blythe, her voice low, recounted incidents of his cruelty towards animals, a sinister harbinger of what was to come.
