### Chapter 7: The Unraveling

With the truth of Thomas’s fate heavy on my heart, I sought out Lillian Grayson, the one person who might help make sense of the darkness enveloping the manor. The time travel brought me to her, a beacon in the fog of uncertainty.

In her study, surrounded by books and the scent of burning sage, Lillian listened intently as I recounted my findings. Her face, usually so composed, reflected the gravity of my words.

"We must act quickly," she said, determination in her voice. "This web of deceit and darkness must be untangled before more lives are lost."

Together, we pored over old newspapers, family records, and Lillian's own notes, tracing the lineage of the family and Damien's troubled past. Each piece of evidence revealed more of the morbid puzzle: unexplained disappearances in the town, strange occurrences at the manor, and Damien's increasingly erratic behavior.

As we delved deeper, a pattern emerged. Each victim had a connection to the manor, and each disappearance coincided with Damien's outbursts of rage or sorrow. It was as if he was the epicenter of a malevolent force, pulling all into his vortex of despair.

But it was the discovery of an old letter in the manor’s archives that brought the final piece into place. Written by Damien's father, it was a confession of sorts, revealing the depths of his son’s disturbance and his own failure to contain it.

With each revelation, the picture became clearer: Damien was not just a troubled soul; he was a predator, masking his true nature behind the façade of a tormented young man.

As the storm outside raged, mirroring our own turbulent emotions, Lillian and I knew that the time had come to confront the darkness. The truth had to be brought to light, no matter the cost.
