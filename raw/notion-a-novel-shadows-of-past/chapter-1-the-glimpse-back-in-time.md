# Chapter 1 The Glimpse Back in Time

- Notion page id: `8677a51c-1360-8357-b8ef-81ebb0abaacd`

In the solitude of the classroom, the only companionship I found was the lingering scent of chalk, a faint echo of the day's lessons, and the soft, gentle tapping of rain against the windows. It had the rhythm of an old song, familiar yet forgotten. I was ensconced at my desk, my gaze sweeping over the disarray of papers scattered before me. However, my mind was far away from the present moment, lost in the labyrinth of memories I could never quite comprehend, let alone attempt to navigate.

In the silence of the room, it happened again. As though a switch had been flipped, the world around me blurred and distorted, the edges of reality fraying and fading like an aged, yellowed photograph left too long in the sun. My heart pounded in my chest like a wild drum, matching the frenetic tempo of my thoughts as I felt the now familiar sensation, akin to falling backward into an abyss, dark and deep.

As the world settled and my heart regained its rhythm, I found myself no longer enclosed within the confines of my classroom. Instead, the air was heavy and thick with the unmistakable aroma of damp cobblestone and the faint, lingering traces of coal smoke. I was in Victorian London, a time and place teeming with life, sprawled out around me like an open book waiting to be read.

As I stumbled through the narrow, winding streets, my attire from the future stood out like a sore thumb, drawing curious and somewhat confused glances from passersby adorned in their petticoats and top hats. I didn’t have a reason why time had chosen to transport me to this era, but experience had been a stern teacher, instructing me to search for the anomaly, the sign that didn't belong in this timeline.

That's when my eyes caught sight of her – the black councillor. She was an anomaly, standing out starkly not just for her attire, which was a striking contrast to the drab, muted colors around her, but for the aura of authority and command she exuded. Her keen, observant eyes scanned the crowd with precision, resting for a fleeting moment on me before moving on.

Compelled by an unseen, inexplicable force, I found myself trailing behind her, my footsteps echoing ominously on the cobblestone, creating a symphony with the sounds of a city alive. She led me to a dimly lit, secluded alley where a young boy, no more than ten years of age, cowered against the cold, brick wall. His eyes were wide and filled with terror.

The councillor knelt before him, her stern, authoritative expression melting away to reveal a softer, more compassionate side. "I know," she whispered, her voice carrying a hint of sorrow that echoed the profound sadness in her eyes.

In the throes of this inexplicable meeting, I felt an overwhelming urge to capture this fleeting moment. Fumbling with the camera I didn't even remember bringing along from my own time, I managed to take a photograph just as the scene began to distort and blur, the edges of this moment in time curling and disintegrating like paper caught in the flame.

In the blink of an eye, I was back in my classroom, the echo of the councillor's empathetic words still lingering in the air, a soft whisper against the backdrop of the rain. The photograph lay in my hands, a tangible link, a bridge between two worlds. It showed the young boy, his eyes wide with fear, and the councillor, her gaze filled with a sorrow so profound, it seemed to transcend time itself.

In that moment, I was certain that this was just the beginning of a journey much larger than myself. The photograph was more than just a piece in a complex puzzle I was destined to solve, it was a mystery that spanned centuries. And so, with the rain still tapping its melancholic rhythm against the window, I began my journey into the uncharted territories of the past.

A sense of urgency settled over me like a cloak as I studied the photograph, the intricate details of the scene etching themselves into my mind. The unknown was calling, its siren song pulling me deeper into the labyrinth of time. I could no longer resist the pull; I was ready to step forward into the mystery that had chosen me.

With a deep breath to steel my nerves, I closed my eyes and reached out to touch the photograph. The instant my fingers brushed against the glossy, smooth surface, I felt a jolt of energy surge through me. The noise of the rain, the familiar soundtrack of my contemporary world, faded into the background, replaced by the distant echo of horse-drawn carriages and hushed whispers.

When I opened my eyes, I was back in the alley with the young boy and the councillor. She was still there, kneeling before him, her comforting words hanging in the air. I watched as she reached out to touch the boy's face, her touch gentle, reassuring, and filled with kindness.

Suddenly, the boy's eyes locked with mine, and I saw a spark of recognition in his gaze. A shiver of realization ran down my spine as I understood that he could see me. I wasn't just an observer of his reality; I was a part of it.

The councillor, noticing the change in the boy's expression, turned to look at me. Our gazes met, and in her eyes, I saw a glimmer of understanding. She knew what I was, a traveler lost in the maze of time.

As abruptly as it had begun, the scene began to fade, the edges of this reality crumbling like a sandcastle against the relentless tide. I tried to reach out, to stay in this moment, but it was too late. The world spun, and I was back in my classroom, the echo of the past still ringing in my ears.

In that moment, I came to the realization that my journey had only just started. The photograph was more than just a piece of the puzzle; it was a key, a portal to another time. And so, with renewed determination and a heart filled with resolve, I pressed on, ready to unravel the threads of the past that had intertwined with my own.
