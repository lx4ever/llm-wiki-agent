### Chapter 3: The Family Secret

I arrived at the grand Manor on a cloudy afternoon, its imposing structure casting long shadows over the well-manicured lawns. Hired to tutor the children of the estate, I was curious about the family, especially after my encounters with the past.

The housekeeper, a stern-faced woman named Mrs. Blythe, greeted me at the door. “The children are in the study,” she said, leading me through the vast corridors lined with portraits of stern-looking ancestors.

In the study, three children sat around a large oak table. Thomas, a boy with bright eyes and a ready smile, greeted me warmly. Elara, with her golden hair and studious gaze, offered a polite nod. And then there was Damien, sitting apart from the others, his face partially hidden by a curtain of dark hair, his eyes glinting with something unreadable.

As we began the lesson, I observed their dynamics. Thomas was quick to answer, eager to please. Elara, though quieter, showed a keen intelligence in her thoughtful responses. Damien, however, remained silent, his presence like a dark cloud in the sunlit room.

After the lesson, I found Elara alone in the garden. “Do you enjoy your lessons, Elara?” I asked.

She looked up, a trace of loneliness in her eyes. “I do, but I miss having friends. My parents don’t let me see many people. They say it’s because of...” Her voice trailed off, her gaze flickering towards the house.

“Because of Damien?” I ventured.

She nodded, biting her lip. “They say he’s troubled, that it’s best if I keep my distance. But he’s always been a part of this house, just like me.”

Her words painted a picture of isolation, a girl trapped in a gilded cage, and a boy shrouded in mystery and misunderstanding.

Later, as I was leaving, I caught a glimpse of Damien, watching us from a window. His expression was unreadable, but in his eyes, I thought I saw a glimmer of something like pain.

As I walked away from the manor, my mind was full of questions. What secrets did this family hold? What role did Damien play in the shadows that seemed to linger over the house?

I knew one thing for certain: to understand the mysteries of the past, I had to unravel the secrets of this family. And to do that, I needed to learn more about Damien, the boy who lived in the shadows.
