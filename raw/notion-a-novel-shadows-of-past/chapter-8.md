### Chapter 8: The Father's Grief

I found Damien’s father in his study, a room shrouded in shadows, much like the man himself. He sat hunched over his desk, his hands trembling as he clutched an old photograph of his son.

"Mr. Alastair," I began, my voice steady despite the turmoil within. "We need to talk about Damien."

He looked up, his eyes haunted by a grief too profound for words. "I've always known," he whispered, his voice breaking. "I've always known something was terribly wrong."

He told me of Damien's childhood, of the incidents that he had brushed aside as mere boyish mischief. But as Damien grew, so did the darkness within him. His father's greatest regret was his refusal to see the truth, blinded by his love and hope for his son.

"I thought his mother's death at the care home had pushed him over the edge. But now...," his voice trailed off, lost in the sea of his guilt.

As he spoke, I realized that Damien's father was another victim, trapped in a prison of his own making, unable to save his son from the monster he had become.

Leaving the broken man to his sorrow, I stepped out into the night, the weight of his confession adding another layer of complexity to the tragedy that had befallen the family.
