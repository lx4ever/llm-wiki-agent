# Prologue

- Notion page id: `df37a51c-1360-83d2-859f-01b5e09d191b`

Word count: 213

As I write this letter, a testament for my descendants or any future reader, I ponder the limitless nature of familial love.

My brother Thomas and I shared a profound bond, as deep as the one we had with Damien, our servant's son. Despite Damien's distorted physical appearance, his heart was pure, and we welcomed him as our own. Yet, societal norms and unspoken prejudices slowly wedged us apart.

Reflecting on those years, I recall an autumn evening when I saw Damien alone, his face hidden in his knees, shoulders trembling in silent sobs against the backdrop of a fiery sunset. It was a poignant reminder of the growing rift among us, my heart heavy with unspoken words and emotions.

Damien's departure from our lives left a void that Thomas filled upon his return from his studies and business endeavors. However, this Thomas was altered, his eyes shadowed by a tragedy that had unfolded. He chose a solitary life, echoing the sorrow that lingered in our family. Despite the changes, he remained my anchor, the sole kin by my side through the years, and I had sworn to protect him as fervently as he did me.

Now, at life's twilight, I am ready to reveal a closely guarded secret, shedding light on the darkness that has long haunted our family. This truth, deeply entwined with the fates of Thomas and Damien, is a burden I can no longer bear alone.

- Elara, December 10th, 1864
