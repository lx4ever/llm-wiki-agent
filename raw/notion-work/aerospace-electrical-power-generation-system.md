# electrical power generation system

- Notion page id: `2317a51c-1360-835f-b213-81cffb22a126`

The electrical power generation system in an aircraft is a critical system that provides the necessary electrical power to operate various aircraft systems, from basic avionics to lighting, heating, navigation, and flight control systems. Here’s an overview of its functions and components:

### Functions of the Electrical Power Generation System

1. Power Supply to Aircraft Systems: Provides power to essential and non-essential systems, including avionics, lighting, climate control, in-flight entertainment, and hydraulic pumps.

1. Battery Charging: Maintains battery levels for emergency backup in case of generator failure.

1. Redundancy and Reliability: Ensures that electrical power is always available even if one or more power sources fail, crucial for flight safety.

1. Power Conversion and Distribution: Converts and distributes power at the appropriate voltage and frequency for different systems on the aircraft.

### How It All Works Together

- When the aircraft engines are running, the primary generators, driven by each engine, generate AC power. This power is distributed via bus bars to operate the aircraft’s systems.

- If the aircraft is on the ground or if there is a need for additional power, the APU can generate power independently.

- The power distribution system ensures that essential systems, such as navigation and flight control, always receive power, while non-essential systems may be powered down during low-power situations.

- In case of a complete engine failure in-flight, the RAT can deploy and generate minimal power to support essential systems and allow the pilot to maintain control and communication.

- Batteries provide emergency power if both the primary and backup generation systems fail, ensuring critical systems remain operational.

The electrical power generation system is essential for flight safety and comfort, supporting not only operational systems but also providing redundancy to maintain functionality under various conditions.
