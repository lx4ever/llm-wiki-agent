### Additional Tips for Saving:

- Book Early: For both flights and Universal Studios tickets, booking in advance will help you lock in lower prices.

- Stay at Hotels with Free Parking and EV Charging: This will save you on both parking fees and charging your Tesla or rental vehicle.

- Mix Meals: Alternate between affordable food trucks and casual restaurants to save on meal costs.

- Use Public Transport for Some Days: You can use LA’s Metro for certain areas like Downtown or Santa Monica to save on gas and parking fees.

### Stay at Budget-Friendly Hotels with Free Parking and EV Charging

- Hotel Options:

- Airbnb or Vacation Rentals: You can find entire apartments or guesthouses for much less than a traditional hotel, especially if you book early. These often come with kitchens, allowing you to save on meals by cooking your own breakfast or lunch.

### Save on Food Costs

- Street Food & Food Trucks: LA is known for its amazing food trucks and street food options, which are both delicious and affordable. You can find everything from tacos to gourmet burgers for much less than at sit-down restaurants.

- In-N-Out Burger: A classic, affordable fast-food option in LA that’s a must-try.

- Happy Hours: Many restaurants and bars offer discounts on food and drinks during happy hour (typically 4-7 PM). You can enjoy great meals and drinks at reduced prices.

- Grocery Store Meals: If you’re staying in an Airbnb or a hotel with a kitchen, you can buy groceries and prepare simple meals to save on dining out.

### Opt for a Self-Guided Beverly Hills Tour

Instead of paying for an organized celebrity homes tour (~$50 per person), you can save by doing a self-guided driving tour. Using apps like Starline’s Star Map or Google Maps, you can drive yourself through famous streets like:

- Mulholland Drive

- Sunset Boulevard

- Benedict Canyon Drive
This allows you to explore at your own pace without the added cost of a tour guide.

### Use Public Transportation or Carpool for Some Days

- Metro: LA’s public transportation system, including the Metro Rail and Metro buses, is affordable and can get you to many attractions (e.g., Santa Monica, Downtown LA). A Metro day pass costs around $7 per person.

- Carpool: If you don’t want to drive every day, consider using UberPool or Lyft Line for cheaper rideshare options when you’re traveling within popular areas like Hollywood, Downtown, and Venice.

### Minimize Parking Costs

Parking fees in LA can add up, especially in busy areas like Hollywood and Santa Monica. To save:

- Book Hotels with Free Parking: Many budget hotels offer free parking, which can save you $20-$40 per day.

- Use Apps: Use parking apps like SpotHero and ParkWhiz to find and reserve cheaper parking spots in advance, especially near tourist areas.

- Street Parking: In many areas, street parking is free or inexpensive. Be mindful of street signs and restrictions, but it’s a good way to save on parking fees.

### Reduce Charging Costs

- Use Hotel EV Chargers: If you stay at hotels with free or low-cost EV chargers, you can significantly reduce the cost of charging your Tesla. Look for hotels offering complimentary Level 2 charging.

- Avoid Peak Charging Hours at Superchargers: Tesla Superchargers often charge different rates based on demand. Charging during off-peak hours can lower your costs.

### Travel During the Off-Season

If you’re flexible with your travel dates, visiting LA during the shoulder season (late September to November or February to early March) can result in lower flight and accommodation prices, while still offering pleasant weather.
