# DC and Boston, April 2025

- Notion page id: `25f7a51c-1360-83f2-afaa-81eac033a4bc`

Here’s the updated itinerary for your road trip from Toronto to Washington, D.C., and Boston, including the driving distances and charging information for your Tesla Model Y:

### Day 1: Toronto to Washington, D.C.

- Driving Distance: ~800 km (~8 hours driving)

- Charging Stops: 1-2 Tesla Supercharger stops, costing approximately $25 CAD in total for the trip down.

- Accommodation: Check into a hotel in Washington, D.C. with EV charging options (e.g., Hyatt Regency Washington on Capitol Hill or Kimpton Hotel Monaco).

### Evening:

- National Mall Night Walk: After arriving, take a relaxing stroll around the National Mall to see the illuminated monuments, including the Lincoln Memorial and the Washington Monument.

- Dinner: Grab a casual dinner at a nearby restaurant (~$40 for two).

Day 1 Total: ~$225 (Hotel, charging, meals)

### Day 2: Smithsonian Museums (Full 6-Hour Visit) & Tidal Basin

### Morning and Early Afternoon:

- Smithsonian Museums: Dedicate at least 6 hours to exploring the Smithsonian Institution, starting with the National Museum of American History and the National Museum of Natural History.

### Lunch:

- Grab a quick bite at the museum cafes or bring a packed lunch (~$30 for two).

### Late Afternoon:

- Tidal Basin and Cherry Blossoms: Head to the Tidal Basin to view the cherry blossoms (if in season) and visit the Jefferson Memorial and the Martin Luther King Jr. Memorial. This is a relaxing way to explore one of the most iconic parts of D.C.

### Evening:

- Dinner in Georgetown: Explore Georgetown, a historic area with great dining options, and enjoy dinner (~$60 for two).

Day 2 Total: ~$150 (Meals, parking)

### Day 3: U.S. Capitol & National Archives

### Morning:

- U.S. Capitol Tour: Start the day with a guided tour of the U.S. Capitol and visit the Library of Congress and Supreme Court nearby.

### Afternoon:

- National Archives: Visit the National Archives to see important historical documents like the Declaration of Independence, the U.S. Constitution, and the Bill of Rights.

### Evening:

- Dinner in Dupont Circle: Enjoy a meal in Dupont Circle, known for its vibrant restaurant scene (~$60 for two).

Day 3 Total: ~$120 (Meals, parking)

### Day 4: Washington, D.C. to Boston

- Driving Distance: ~700 km (~7 hours driving)

- Charging Stops: 1-2 Tesla Supercharger stops, costing about $25 CAD for the drive.

- Accommodation: Check into a hotel in Boston with EV charging options (e.g., The Westin Copley Place or The Colonnade Hotel).

### Evening:

- Quincy Market: After arriving in Boston, head to Quincy Market for dinner and explore the nearby Faneuil Hall Marketplace (~$50 for two).

Day 4 Total: ~$225 (Hotel, charging, meals)

### Day 5: Explore Boston's Freedom Trail

### Morning and Early Afternoon:

- Freedom Trail: Walk the Freedom Trail, a 2.5-mile (4 km) trail through historic sites such as Boston Common, Paul Revere's House, and Old North Church. It's a great way to experience the revolutionary history of the city.

### Lunch:

- Stop for lunch at a nearby cafe or quick stop along the Freedom Trail (~$30 for two).

### Evening:

- Dinner in the North End: End the day with Italian food in Boston's North End, a historic and vibrant neighborhood (~$60 for two).

Day 5 Total: ~$150 (Meals, parking)

### Day 6: Harvard & MIT

### Morning:

- Harvard University: Drive to Cambridge and spend the morning exploring the Harvard University campus, including Harvard Yard and the Harvard Art Museums.

### Lunch:

- Have lunch at a cafe in Cambridge (~$30 for two).

### Afternoon:

- MIT Museum: Visit the MIT Museum, which offers fascinating exhibits on science and innovation (~$12 per person).

### Evening:

- Boston Public Garden: Stroll through the beautiful Boston Public Garden, free to explore and a perfect way to wind down.

- Dinner: Casual dining near Boston Common (~$60 for two).

Day 6 Total: ~$150 (Meals, activities, parking)

### Day 7: Boston to Toronto

- Driving Distance: ~900 km (~9 hours driving)

- Charging Stops: 1-2 Tesla Supercharger stops (~$30 CAD for the return trip).

### Lunch:

- Have lunch on the road (~$30 for two).

### Evening:

- Arrival in Toronto: Return home in the evening.

Day 7 Total: ~$60 (Meals, charging)

### How to Find EV Charging Hotels:

You can easily locate hotels with EV charging by using these resources:

- Tesla Destination Charging: Check Tesla's official map of destination chargers (hotels, restaurants, etc.) on their website or directly through the Tesla app.

- PlugShare: A great resource for finding EV charging stations, including hotels with Level 2 and Tesla chargers.

- Hotel Booking Sites (Filter for EV Charging): Sites like Booking.com and Expedia allow you to filter results by "Electric Vehicle Charging Station" under amenities.
