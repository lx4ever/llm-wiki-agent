- My self-awarness this morning: I was a bit angry when I saw that the toilet paper has ran out and my husband did not change it. I actually “caught” myself feeling the anger, and then immediately extended my anger to his action of “not putting a new roll” to his intention of “not caring for other and being lazy” then to “he is a selfish and lazy person who doesn't care about others”. Then I suddenly made the realization that I am “labelling” his one action as his whole being. Which is extremely unfair because I KNOW that he is a caring person, and I KNOW that he can be lazy at doing somethings but it is not that he has meant any harm or being selfish. Lazy ≠ Selfish or bad character. Bad habits ≠ bad intentions/character. Once I caught myself doing this, I tried to step out of my mind and see this as a third person perspective, try to let my anger and resentment flow through my mind and just observe these feelings without letting them impact me too much. I wasn’t doing it properly but I did feel that self-awareness has improved significantly since I started medication and learning about awareness. Thank you and I am actually much happier and I feel like a better person as well.
